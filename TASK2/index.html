About Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking (1942â€“2018) was a renowned theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who made groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of black holes and the nature of the universe. Despite being diagnosed with ALS at the age of 21 and living with the condition for over five decades, he defied odds and continued his work using advanced communication technology. His bestselling book, A Brief History of Time, brought complex scientific ideas to a global audience. Hawking's life and work remain a testament to human resilience, intellect, and curiosity about the cosmos.

Major Contributions

  • Developed the theory of Hawking Radiation, explaining how black holes emit radiation.
  • Contributed to the unification of general relativity and quantum mechanics.
  • Authored several popular science books, including The Universe in a Nutshell.
